    Below is a quick selection of properties Jewell Hill has managed for its clients in recent history. There is no project too big or too small for Jewell Hill to assist your financial institution in making sure your real estate assets are protected until a suitable buyer can be located.

    Troubled Mall

    The Steeplegate Mall in Concord, New Hampshire was foreclosed upon by a New York-based commercial bank in 2014. Jewell Hill oversaw the management of the property during the bank's ownership of the mall, increasing tenancy by over 20% despite troubled economic conditions in the once-flourishing commercial district of Concord. The property was agressively marketed by Jewell Hill's commercial brokerage partner and was sold in 2015.

    Roadside Motel

    The Shediac Motel along the Trans-Canada Highway in Shediac, New Brunswick was foreclosed upon by a Montreal-based commercial bank in 2005. Jewell Hill oversaw the management of the property during the bank's ownership of the motel, successfully completing a number of capital improvements and retaining the services of management staff who maintained the motel's operations until the property was sold by Jewell Hill's commercial brokerage partner in 2010.

    Historic Office Building

    The historic and iconic Time & Temperature Building in downtown Portland, Maine was foreclosed upon by a Chicago-based commercial bank in 2011. Jewell Hill oversaw the management of the property curing the bank's ownership, increased tenancy and completed necessary capital improvements. The property was sold by Jewell Hill's commercial brokerage partner in 2012.

    Residential Property

    In addition to commercial properties, Jewell Hill also has extensive experience and ample capacity of manage, maintain, repair and market residential properties of all sizes and configurations. Jewell Hill maintains relationships with residential real estate agents throughout New England and Eastern Canada in order to ensure the fastest possible resale.
